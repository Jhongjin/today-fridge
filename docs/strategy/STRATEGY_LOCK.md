# STRATEGY_LOCK

## 1. One Sentence

`오늘의 냉장고` is a Toss Apps in Toss H5 casual game where women in their 40s-50s clean a fridge, rescue leftover ingredients, collect home-meal recipes, and compete on a daily score.

## 2. North Star

Players should briefly return every day and feel:

> "I cleaned today's fridge, saved ingredients, made a meal, and I can beat that score tomorrow."

### North Star Metric

Daily completed fridge rounds.

This counts completed core play, not simple visits or reward clicks.

## 3. Target

### Primary

Women in their 40s-50s who understand shopping, leftovers, meal prep, fridge organization, and food waste as familiar daily contexts.

Likely preferences:

- Simple touch play.
- Familiar household themes.
- Clear progress and visible cleanup.
- Light competition with friends or similar users.
- Short sessions with satisfying closure.

### Secondary

Toss users looking for a quick reward-friendly casual game inside Toss.

## 4. Positioning

Lifestyle-framed casual puzzle:

- Not a cooking simulator.
- Not a generic match-3 clone.
- Not a pure reward-ad game.
- Not a real fridge-management utility.

The game turns the familiar act of fridge cleanup into a short, repeatable puzzle with visible order, recipe collection, and fair daily competition.

## 5. Differentiation

- Household theme: fridge, leftovers, expiration, home meals.
- Cleanup satisfaction: messy fridge becomes orderly.
- Recipe collection: cleared ingredients turn into recipe cards.
- Fair competition: everyone plays the same daily fridge seed.
- Toss-fit session: one complete play in 60-90 seconds for MVP.

## 6. Non-Negotiables

- The first playable action must be understood in 5 seconds.
- One round must feel complete in 60-90 seconds.
- Touch controls must stay simple: tap, select, move, confirm.
- The fridge cleanup fantasy must remain central.
- Competition must be clear but not punishing.
- Ranked competition must not become pay-to-win.
- Readability beats decoration.
- Tone must respect the target; cute is fine, childish is not.
- Toss point promotions must not depend on score, rank, win/loss, probability, or random rewards.

## 7. Do Not Build

- Full cooking simulation.
- Real grocery/fridge management app.
- Heavy story or kitchen renovation meta.
- Complex ingredient database.
- Random gacha economy.
- Ranking rewards tied to money or Toss points.
- Long recipe text reading.
- High-pressure timer-first gameplay.
- Tiny ingredients, tiny buttons, or flashy effects that hurt readability.

## 8. Core Loop

1. Open today's fridge.
2. See the daily cleanup goal and recipe target.
3. Select ingredients from fridge shelves into a limited prep tray.
4. Clear three identical ingredients or complete a recipe combination.
5. Rescue expiring ingredients for bonus score.
6. Complete a dish and submit `오늘의 냉파 점수`.
7. Check personal, friend, or daily ranking.
8. Replay to improve routing, combo, and waste score.

## 9. Key Metrics

### Activation

- First round start rate.
- Tutorial completion rate.
- First round completion rate.

### Engagement

- Daily completed fridge rounds.
- Plays per user per day.
- Replay rate after first completion.
- Leaderboard view rate.

### Retention

- D1 retention.
- D3 retention.
- D7 retention.
- Return after daily mission completion.

### Collection

- Recipe card acquisition rate.
- Recipe book reopen rate.
- Replay rate driven by missing recipe cards.

### Competition

- Leaderboard submission rate.
- Friend challenge open rate.
- Replay after ranking view.
- Personal best improvement attempts.

## 10. Decision Test

Every major feature must pass these questions:

1. Does it strengthen fridge cleanup?
2. Can the target player understand it quickly?
3. Does it preserve short-session simplicity?
4. Does it improve replay, collection, or fair competition?
5. Does it comply with Toss platform constraints?

If three or more answers are no, the feature goes to backlog or redesign.

## 11. Locked Direction

`오늘의 냉장고` translates fridge cleanup into the simplest possible game language. The main value is not complex cooking. It is the immediate satisfaction of organizing a messy fridge, saving ingredients, completing a familiar meal, and wanting one more fair attempt at a better score.
