# BGM_DIRECTION

## Goal

The background music should make the game feel warm, tidy, and replayable without creating timer anxiety.

## Target Mood

- Korean home kitchen, light and familiar.
- Calm casual puzzle, not arcade pressure.
- Friendly enough for daily replay, but not childish.
- Low-fatigue for repeated 1-3 minute sessions.

## Musical Palette

- Tempo: 78-92 BPM.
- Instruments: soft marimba, muted acoustic guitar, light piano, brush percussion, warm bass.
- Texture: small ensemble, sparse melody, no dense high-frequency loops.
- Key feel: major or gentle modal color; avoid dramatic minor tension.

## Loop Spec

- Loop length: 45-75 seconds.
- Loop point must be seamless and quiet.
- Intro should be under 2 seconds or start directly in-loop.
- Mix target should sit below SFX; SFX must remain readable.

## State Treatment

- Normal play: one calm loop.
- Pause: BGM should duck or stop cleanly; no suspense bed.
- Result: use SFX flourish first; BGM can continue softly underneath.
- Failure: no scolding sting, only a short soft notice.

## Avoid

- Casino-like reward motifs.
- Fast countdown percussion.
- Child toy melodies.
- Sharp bells or high repeating chimes.
- Vocals, lyrics, or recognizable copyrighted melodies.

## Asset Requirements

- Source files must have commercial usage rights or be original.
- Keep final assets small enough to preserve the mobile bundle budget.
- Provide at least OGG and MP3/AAC fallback if real audio assets are added.
- BGM must respect mute and future separate BGM/SFX controls.
